Welcome to the Roof of Western Europe

The French Alps stretch across southeast France, crowned by Mont Blanc (4,809 m) — Western Europe's highest peak. This legendary range weaves together world-class skiing, storybook mountain villages, and a culinary heritage shaped by centuries of alpine life. In winter, endless powder fields and perfectly groomed slopes draw enthusiasts from around the globe. When summer arrives, the same mountains transform into a vast playground of wildflower meadows, crystal-clear lakes, and trails that wind through some of the most dramatic scenery on the continent.

Whether you seek the adrenaline of a first descent, the quiet of a lakeside morning, or the warmth of a fireside fondue, the Alps meet you exactly where you are — and then gently push you further. (France.fr, 2026; Wikipedia, 2026)

Top 3 Destinations

Each a distinct expression of alpine majesty.

Chamonix‑Mont‑Blanc
Cradle of mountaineering (first ascent in 1786) and host of the 1924 Winter Olympics. Ride the Aiguille du Midi cable car for panoramic views, hike the legendary Tour du Mont Blanc, or step into the glass skywalk “Step into the Void.” (Wikipedia, 2026)
Annecy
The “Venice of the Alps.” Crystal‑clear lake, canals, and the romantic Pont des Amours. Explore the old town and visit the castle for a stunning lake view. (Wikipedia, 2026)
Courchevel
Luxury skiing at its finest. Home to 7 Michelin‑starred restaurants and perfectly groomed slopes in the world's largest ski area. (Wikipedia, 2026)

Year‑Round Activities

From powder slopes to blooming meadows — adventure awaits in every season.

Winter

Ski in top resorts like Val d'Isère and Alpe d'Huez, where the legendary 16‑km "Sarenne" run offers one of the longest uninterrupted descents in the Alps. Beyond the pistes, try snowshoeing, ice climbing, or simply relax in a mountain refuge. The season runs from December to late March. (France.fr, 2026)

Alpine Cuisine

In the mountains, food is ritual and reward. Alpine cuisine draws from a deep tradition: rich cheeses from high-pasture milk, hearty dishes for cold days, and liqueurs distilled from wild herbs. Every meal tells a story of the landscape. (Wikipedia, 2026)

  • 🫕 Fondue Savoyarde rich melted cheese with crusty bread
  • 🧀 Raclette scraped cheese over potatoes & charcuterie
  • 🏔️ Local Cheeses Beaufort, Reblochon, Tomme de Savoie
  • 🫐 Blueberry Tart wild blueberries from alpine meadows
  • 🥃 Génépi herbal liqueur, the perfect digestif

Practical Tips

A little planning goes a long way in the Alps.

📅

Best Time to Visit

  • Winter (Dec–Mar): Reliable snow, lively resorts, skiing & snowboarding.
  • Summer (Jun–Sep): Warm days for hiking, cycling, lake swimming. July–August peak.
  • Shoulder seasons (May–Jun & Sep–Oct): Fewer crowds, lower prices, wildflowers or golden larches.
  • High-mountain lifts operate mid-June to mid-September; check before planning.
(France.fr, 2026)
🏨

Book Accommodation Early

  • Peak times: Christmas, New Year, February holidays fill up fast.
  • Book 3–6 months ahead for best choice and rates.
  • Lakefront hotels in Annecy are especially sought after in summer.
  • Shoulder seasons offer more availability and often a warmer welcome.
  • Check local tourist office sites for family-run chalets not on major platforms.
(France.fr, 2026)
🚂

Getting Around with Ease

  • TGV Paris–Annecy ~3.5 hrs; regional train to Chamonix ~2.5 hrs.
  • Route des Grandes Alpes: scenic drive, open Jun–Oct, crosses Col de l'Iseran (2,764 m).
  • Mountain passes may close outside summer; always check conditions.
  • In resorts, use cable cars, funiculars, and free shuttle buses (e.g., Chamonix).
(France.fr, 2026)
