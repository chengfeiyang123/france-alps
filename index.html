France Alps

Welcome to the Roof of Western Europe

The French Alps stretch across southeast France, with Mont Blanc (4,809 m) as Western Europe's highest peak. This region offers world‑class skiing, charming mountain towns, exquisite cuisine, and outdoor activities for every season.

Whether you seek thrill, relaxation, or gastronomy, the Alps deliver an unforgettable experience.

Top 3 Destinations

Each a distinct expression of alpine majesty.

Chamonix‑Mont‑Blanc
Cradle of mountaineering (first ascent in 1786) and host of the 1924 Winter Olympics. Ride the Aiguille du Midi cable car for panoramic views, hike the legendary Tour du Mont Blanc, or step into the glass skywalk “Step into the Void.”
Annecy
The “Venice of the Alps.” Crystal‑clear lake, canals, and the romantic Pont des Amours. Explore the old town and visit the castle for a stunning lake view.
Courchevel
Luxury skiing at its finest. Home to 7 Michelin‑starred restaurants and perfectly groomed slopes in the world's largest ski area.

Year‑Round Activities

From powder slopes to blooming meadows — adventure awaits in every season.

Winter

Ski in Val d'Isère, Alpe d'Huez, 16‑km “Sarenne” run.

Summer

Hike meadows, cycle passes, paddle Annecy Lake.

Autumn

Golden larches, quiet trails, fewer crowds.

Spring

Blossoming meadows, mild weather, renewal.
